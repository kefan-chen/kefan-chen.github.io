Job Market Paper

Growth at the Margin: Political Incentives and Firm Behavior in China
Sole author, September 2025
This paper examines how China’s annual GDP growth targets—an essential feature of its economic governance—shape incentives for city leaders and influence firm-level output and resource allocation. Using survival models, bunching analysis, and a threshold-based strategy, I find that a one-unit increase in a city leader’s performance score raises the probability of promotion by 9–10%. The analysis reveals a significant clustering of performance scores just above the growth threshold, and at the firm level, GDP-related indicators show discontinuities that reflect real production responses.
